# Student Performance & Lifestyle Analysis 📊🎓

## 📌 Overview
This project investigates **student academic performance** by analyzing both **demographic factors** and **lifestyle behaviors** through interactive dashboards.

The project uses **data visualization and storytelling techniques** to answer:
- Who performs differently?
- Why do these differences occur?
- What are the key drivers of student success?

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze student performance using multiple factors
- Identify at-risk student groups
- Understand how lifestyle and behavior affect academic outcomes
- Present insights through interactive dashboards

---

## 📊 Project Structure

The analysis is divided into **three dashboards**:

---

## 📌 Dashboard 1: Performance Segmentation

Focus: **Who performs differently?**



### Key Insights:
- Students with **low attendance and low study time** are most at risk
- Higher attendance leads to more stable performance
- Parental education has **limited influence**
- Most students are aged **15–18**

### Visualizations Used:
- 100% Stacked Bar Chart
- Box Plot
- Pie Chart
- Clustered Column Chart

---

## 📌 Dashboard 2: Lifestyle & Behavior Analysis

Focus: **Why performance differences occur**



### Key Insights:
- **Engagement, sleep, and stress** significantly affect performance
- High performers maintain better study habits
- No strong trade-off between lifestyle and academic success
- Correlations between variables are generally weak

### Visualizations Used:
- Line Charts
- Clustered Bar Charts
- Scatter Plots
- Heatmaps

---

## 📌 Dashboard 3: Key Drivers & Summary

Focus: **What drives success?**



### Key Insights:
- **Study intensity** is the strongest predictor of performance
- **Engagement** is the second most important factor
- Attendance strongly influences academic risk
- Some students maintain high performance despite stress

### Visualizations Used:
- Decomposition Tree
- Column Chart
- Donut Chart
- Line Chart

---

## 🛠 Data Preparation

### Features Used:
- Demographics: Gender, Age, SES, Parental Education
- Academic: Grades, Study Hours, Attendance
- Behavioral: Stress, Sleep, Lifestyle, Engagement

### Derived Features:
- Final Score (G1, G2, G3 average)
- Attendance categories
- Lifestyle Score
- Study Intensity
- Engagement Score
- Performance categories (Low / Medium / High)
